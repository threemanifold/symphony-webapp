# Streams of a Small Symphony

A two-room house upon the disk —
on one side, Python keeps the books
(SQLite, three columns and a risk:
no auth, no migrations, only hooks
that wake when uvicorn arrives
and tuck each conversation in
beside the timestamp of its lives,
the title it begins within).

Across the hall, the other room
is bright with Vite and React state:
a sidebar list, a search, a loom
of bubbles waiting to translate
the token-stream the server sends —
each `data:` a heartbeat on the wire,
each `[ERROR]` a thread that ends
before the model speaks entire.

Between them runs a thin protocol,
relative routes, event-stream prose;
Anthropic answers from the call,
the assistant bubble overflows,
and when the final chunk has flown
the backend signs the ledger clean:
the user message, fully grown,
the reply, and the time between.

Above it all, the orchestra —
Symphony, Codex, Claude, the rest —
turns tickets into PRs, a
quiet choreography of test
and lint and push and squash and land,
of workpads kept in plain markdown,
of branches cut by a careful hand,
of `OK:` shouted, calm, not loud.

So here is what the repo is:
a template, but a working one;
a chat that streams, a quiz, a quiz
of patterns we'll repeat — undone
and redone — each new SYM-
a stanza in a longer score,
each merge a measure, every limb
a small new instrument to score.

Sing softly, then, repository:
your tests are green, your readme spare;
your purpose is not history
but rehearsal — air, and air, and air.
